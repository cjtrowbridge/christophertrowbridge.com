What Am I Working On?

I'm a senior at SFSU studying Urban Studies with minors in Race and Resistance Studies and Queer Ethnic Studies. I'm focusing on sustainable urban infrastructure, particularly in experimental and improvised communities.

I currently work in disaster response, providing critical infrastructure to communities in need. I also moonlight as an educator helping communities develop their sustainable infrastructure and subsistence farming techniques.

I plan to expand this work after I graduate, helping communities build the housing, food, and other infrastructure they need to survive and thrive in a changing world.

High Ground

This is an evolving project which aims to bridge the gap between the thousands of permaculture communities across the nation and those who wish to learn the skills needed to join those communities as valuable members.

It will be a research institute which works on R&D for sustainable alternative infrastructure, and on educating visitors and the public at large about these solutions.

Anyone will be able to come and learn the fundamentals for each of the major categories of community infrastructure, free of charge.

Stay tuned; more info will be posted soon.

Future Projects

Community Capital

One of my greatest long-term aspirations is to open a venture capital firm dedicated to ethics, justice, and sustainability. I like the phrase "Community Capital" which seems to convey these points while also conveying a dedication to both crowd funding and crowd sourcing.

There are many examples of socially conscious VC funds, but none that I know of is also focused on sustainability. There is a new perspective developing at the intersection of business and sociology which argues that sustainable and ethical business practices are better investments.

In the case of the Vanguard ESGV ETF, their portfolio focuses on companies which value best practices in environmental, social, and corporate governance (ESG). This is a good example where the argument ESG is making is that these ESG-conscious companies will outperform other companies.

I plan to eventually start a VC firm which focuses on ESG investments and helps companies develop into better ESG citizens. Putting out information about this topic will also be an excellent way to lead social change while drawing in further investment.

Past Projects

Desolation

I recently spent several weeks in the desolation wilderness (what an appropriate name) and I developed a manifesto combining intentional community, permaculture, and arcology with the goal of creating communities designed to survive and thrive during the ongoing collapse of the biosphere.

The world is turning into a desert. Studies project that most of the US will be desert within the next few decades. I embrace the desert and xericulture or agriculture designed for deserts. If the world is turning into a desert, then learning to embrace self-sufficience in the desert now will give communities an advantage during the difficult times on the horizon. I argue that there are actually advantages in terms of self-sufficiency for communities in the desert.

Read the Desolation Manifesto

Moving forward, I intend to buy hundreds of acres of land in the high desert to found such a community sometime in the next several years. Stay tuned for more info.
