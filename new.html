What Am I Working On?

I'm a senior at SFSU studying Urban Studies with minors in Race and Resistance Studies and Queer Ethnic Studies. I'm focusing on sustainable urban infrastructure, particularly in experimental and improvised communities.

I currently work in disaster response, providing critical infrastructure to communities in need. I also moonlight as an educator helping communities develop their sustainable infrastructure and subsistence farming techniques.

I plan to expand this work after I graduate, helping communities build the housing, food, and other infrastructure they need to survive and thrive in a changing world.

High Ground

This is an evolving project which aims to bridge the gap between the thousands of permaculture communities across the nation and those who wish to learn the skills needed to join those communities as valuable members.

It will be a research institute which works on R&D for sustainable alternative infrastructure, and on educating visitors and the public at large about these solutions.

Anyone will be able to come and learn the fundamentals for each of the major categories of community infrastructure, free of charge.

Stay tuned; more info will be posted soon.

Burners Without Borders SF

I cofounded the first ever SF Bay Area chapter of Burners Without Borders! Ironically though the international network is headquartered here, they have not yet had an active chapter here.

The era of covid has been a struggle. Initially we launched three projects and held public meetings to get them started, but because of the many overlapping disasters, we have been forced to refactor.

We will be holding a public zoom session soon with more information about relaunching the projects and how you can plug in!
